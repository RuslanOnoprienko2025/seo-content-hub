## Why Logo Create Problems

Email signatures with logo elements seem professional. Company logos signal brand identity. Visual elements create polish. In theory, this makes sense.

In practice, logos create more problems than they solve.

### Image Blocking Breaks Logo

When your logo signature arrives in someone's inbox, they see:

- Empty box where the logo should appear
- "Download images" security warning
- Broken layout with missing visual element
- Unprofessional appearance despite your design investment

Most recipients never click "download images"—especially for unfamiliar senders. Your expensive logo becomes a liability.

### Logos Trigger Spam Filters

Every external image request increases spam scoring. Your logo file lives on a server. Spam filters detect external requests and raise suspicion—particularly for high-volume senders.

Sales teams, account managers, and support staff sending dozens of daily emails with logo signature elements see measurably worse inbox placement than colleagues using text-only signatures.

## Layout Patterns That Work

Instead of creative custom design, follow proven email signature layout patterns.

### Vertical Format

Stack each element on separate lines:

```
Jennifer Martinez
Senior Account Manager
CloudTech Solutions
(555) 234-5678
jennifer.martinez@cloudtech.com
cloudtech.com
```

This format maximizes readability, works flawlessly on mobile, and renders consistently across all platforms. No creativity required—just logical information sequence.

### Inline Format

Combine elements horizontally for space efficiency:

```
Jennifer Martinez | Senior Account Manager at CloudTech Solutions
(555) 234-5678
jennifer.martinez@cloudtech.com
cloudtech.com
```

Conserves vertical space while maintaining clarity. Still mobile-responsive. Still universally compatible.

Both patterns solve the layout challenge without specialized expertise. Choose based on preference—both deliver reliable results.
